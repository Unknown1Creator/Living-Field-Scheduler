## 1. **System Pressure Field**

The state of the system is represented by a 6-dimensional pressure vector:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) = 
\begin{bmatrix}
P_{\text{cpu}}(t) \\
P_{\text{mem}}(t) \\
P_{\text{io}}(t) \\
P_{\text{net}}(t) \\
P_{\text{fs}}(t) \\
P_{\text{energy}}(t)
\end{bmatrix}
\in \mathbb{R}^6
\]

Each component is clamped to the interval \([0.0, 9.5]\) after every update:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \text{clamp}(\mathbf{P}(t), \, 0.0, \, 9.5)
\]

---

## 2. **Global Pressure Score**

The scalar system pressure is computed via a weighted sum:

\[
S_{\text{global}}(t) = \mathbf{w}^T \mathbf{P}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{6} w_i P_i(t)
\]

where the weight vector is:

\[
\mathbf{w} = [1.40, \, 1.15, \, 1.05, \, 0.85, \, 0.75, \, 0.90]
\]

---

## 3. **Holistic Compression (Equilibrium Pull)**

The pressure field is updated toward the global mean \(\mu(t)\) with adaptive compression factor \(\alpha(S_{\text{global}})\):

\[
\mu(t) = \frac{1}{6} \sum_{i=1}^{6} P_i(t)
\]

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \alpha(S_{\text{global}}) \cdot \mathbf{P}(t) + (1 - \alpha(S_{\text{global}})) \cdot \mu(t) \cdot \mathbf{1}
\]

where \(\alpha(S)\) is an adaptive compression factor, typically in \([0.78, 0.85]\), increasing with \(S_{\text{global}}\).

---

## 4. **Quantum Entanglement Pull (QEP)**

Real-time tasks are pulled toward high-pressure regions via entanglement:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \mathbf{P}(t) + \lambda_{\text{entangle}}(S_{\text{global}}) \cdot S_{\text{global}} \cdot \mathbf{D}_t
\]

where \(\lambda_{\text{entangle}}(S)\) is the entanglement strength, typically in \([0.4, 0.6]\), increasing with \(S_{\text{global}}\).

---

## 5. **Quantum Interference (Conflict Resolution)**

Interference reduces pressure in overlapping resource demands:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \mathbf{P}(t) + \gamma_{\text{interfere}} \cdot \text{Interfere}(\mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t))
\]

where:

\[
\text{Interfere}(\mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t)) = \sum_{j \neq t} \left( \text{overlap}(\mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{D}_j) \cdot \text{phase}(\mathbf{P}_j) \right)
\]

\(\text{overlap}(\cdot)\) measures demand similarity; \(\text{phase}(\cdot)\) encodes resource conflict phase.

---

## 6. **Quantum Decoherence (Lazy Preemption)**

Idle or low-priority tasks apply noise to simulate PREEMPT_LAZY:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \mathbf{P}(t) + \nu_{\text{decohere}} \cdot \text{noise}(\mathbf{P}(t))
\]

where \(\nu_{\text{decohere}}\) is adaptive based on task age and pressure.

---

## 7. **Quantum Tunneling (Escape from Local Minima)**

Background tasks escape local pressure minima:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \mathbf{P}(t) + \mu_{\text{tunnel}} \cdot \exp\left(-\frac{E(\mathbf{P}(t))}{k_B T}\right) \cdot \text{random\_walk}(\mathbf{P}(t))
\]

where \(E(\mathbf{P})\) is pressure energy, \(k_B\) is Boltzmann constant, \(T\) is system temperature (adapted from pressure).

---

## 8. **Strain Field Calculation (Compression Kernel Integration)**

For each task \(t\), the strain is computed as:

\[
S(x) = r(x) + \alpha \cdot C_d(x) + \beta \cdot E_h(x)
\]

where:

- \(r(x) = \frac{|\text{compressed}(x)|}{|\text{original}(x)|}\) — compression ratio of task state
- \(C_d(x) = \|\mathbf{D}_t - \mathbf{P}(t)\|_2\) — decompression tension (pressure deviation)
- \(E_h(x) = \text{moving\_avg}(S(x), k)\) — historical entropy over \(k\) ticks
- \(\alpha = 0.3\), \(\beta = 0.2\) — tunable weights

---

## 9. **Inverse Entropy Echo (IEE) Shadow Creation**

For high-strain tasks (\(S(x) > \tau_2 = 0.7\)):

\[
S_{\text{echo}}(x) = \left| r(x) - r(\text{reverse}(x)) \right|
\]

\[
\mathbf{P}_{\text{shadow}} \leftarrow \text{reverse}(\mathbf{P}(t))
\]

---

## 10. **Collapse Gain (Shadow Optimization)**

When a new state \(y\) is selected:

\[
\text{CollapseGain}(y) = \max(0, 1 - |S(y) - S_{\text{echo}}(x)|)
\]

If \(\text{CollapseGain}(y) > 0\), collapse the shadow ? update pressure field.

---

## 11. **Dictionary Learning Trigger (Emergent Adaptation)**

If average strain over \(k\) ticks is below threshold \(\theta = 0.6\):

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \mathbf{P}(t) + \text{dict\_learn}(\mathbf{P}(t))
\]

where \(\text{dict\_learn}\) is a lightweight dictionary compression of pressure state.

---

## 12. **Resistance Detection (Diagnostic)**

For each task \(t\):

\[
R(x) = S(x) - S_{\text{baseline}}(x)
\]

where \(S_{\text{baseline}}(x)\) is a 100-tick rolling average of strain.

If \(R(x) > 0\), flag as “resistance” ? trigger damping.

## 14. **Self-Organizing Pressure Clustering (SOPC)**

During pressure field update:

\[
\mathbf{P}(t) \leftarrow \mathbf{P}(t) + \kappa_{\text{self-org}} \cdot \left[ \text{density}(\mathbf{D}_t) \cdot \text{attract}(\mathbf{D}_t) \right]
\]

where:

- \(\text{density}(\mathbf{D}_t) = \text{norm}(\mathbf{D}_t)\)
- \(\text{attract}(\mathbf{D}_t) = \cos\theta(\mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t))\)

? Tasks cluster by demand similarity ? reduces pressure field noise.

## 15. **Energy-Aware Strain Compression (EASC)**

For energy-aware scheduling:

\[
\text{progress}_t \leftarrow r_{\text{base}} \cdot f(S_{\text{global}}, \mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t)) \cdot \text{energy\_weight}(\mathbf{P}(t))
\]

where:

\[
\text{energy\_weight}(\mathbf{P}) = \exp\left(-\frac{P_{\text{energy}}}{\text{avg\_energy}}\right)
\]

? Prioritizes tasks with low energy demand ? reduces system energy consumption.

## 16. **Task Demand and State Model**

Each task \(t\) maintains:

\[
\mathbf{D}_t \in \mathbb{R}^6, \quad \|\mathbf{D}_t\|_1 = 1, \quad D_{t,i} \geq 0
\]

Progress: \(\text{progress}_t \in [0, 1]\)  
Age: \(\text{age}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0\)

---

## 17. **Task Scheduling Score**

The scheduler selects tasks with lowest score:

\[
\text{score}(t) = \|\, k \cdot \mathbf{D}_t - \mathbf{P}(t) \,\|_2 + c(S_{\text{global}}) \cdot (1 - \text{progress}_t) - \beta(S_{\text{global}}) \cdot \text{age}_t
\]

where:

- \(k = 4.1\) — demand scaling factor
- \(c(S)\) — urgency gain (increases with \(S_{\text{global}}\))
- \(\beta(S)\) — anti-starvation gain (increases with \(S_{\text{global}}\))

---

## 18. **Task Progress Update (Modulation Function)**

During execution:

\[
\Delta \text{progress}_t = r_{\text{base}} \cdot f(S_{\text{global}}, \mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t))
\]

where:

\[
f(S_{\text{global}}, \mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t)) = \max\left(0.32, \, 2.45 - 0.155 \cdot S_{\text{global}}\right) \cdot \left(1 + \gamma \cdot \cos\theta(\mathbf{D}_t, \mathbf{P}(t))\right)
\]

- \(\gamma = 0.18\) — alignment bonus
- \(\cos\theta(\mathbf{D}, \mathbf{P}) = \frac{\mathbf{D} \cdot \mathbf{P}}{\|\mathbf{D}\| \cdot \|\mathbf{P}\|}\)

---

## 19. **Completion Relief Propagation**

Upon task completion (\(\text{progress}_t \geq 1\)):

\[
\delta_{\text{relief}} = -1.25 \cdot \|\mathbf{D}_t\|_1 \cdot \phi(\mathbf{D}_t)
\]

where:

\[
\phi(\mathbf{D}_t) = \sum_{i=1}^{6} D_{t,i}^2
\]

Apply non-local propagation with \(\delta_{\text{relief}}\).
